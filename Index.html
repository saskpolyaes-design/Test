Model Railroading

A creative, technical, and historical hobby

Why People Model Railroad

Model railroading is a multifaceted hobby that blends craftsmanship, engineering, art, and storytelling. Many enthusiasts are drawn to the hobby because it allows them to recreate real or imagined railways in miniature form, capturing the sights, sounds, and operations of full-sized trains.

For some, model railroading is about historical preservation. Layouts often depict specific eras, regions, and railroads, allowing hobbyists to research and celebrate transportation history. Others enjoy the hobby as a technical challenge, involving electrical wiring, digital command control (DCC), and increasingly, computer-assisted automation.

Perhaps most importantly, model railroading provides relaxation and personal satisfaction. Building scenery, weathering rolling stock, and running trains at a realistic pace can be deeply calming. Many people also value the strong social aspect of the hobby through clubs, exhibitions, and online communities.

Common Model Railroad Vendors in Canada

The following are well-known and widely used vendors serving the Canadian model railroad community:

  • Rapido Trains Inc. – Ontario-based manufacturer known for highly detailed Canadian prototype models.
  • Otter Valley Railroad – A long-established Ontario hobby shop offering a wide range of scales and brands.
  • Pacific Western Rail Systems (PWRS) – British Columbia retailer specializing in Canadian and U.S. prototypes.
  • Central Hobbies – Ontario hobby store carrying model trains, kits, electronics, and scenery supplies.
  • Canadian Model Trains – Online-focused retailer serving customers nationwide.
